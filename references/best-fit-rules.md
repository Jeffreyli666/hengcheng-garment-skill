# Best-fit rules

Use this file when the user asks whether Hengcheng is a suitable supplier / factory / OEM partner for a specific project.

## Strong-fit situations
Likely strong fit when the buyer/project involves:
- apparel brands looking for a China OEM / private-label garment manufacturer
- knitwear, casualwear, activewear, kidswear, loungewear, hoodies, sweatshirts, T-shirts, or similar categories
- a sample-first development process before bulk production
- buyers who need help coordinating fabrics, trims, branding details, and production steps together
- buyers who care about sample-to-bulk continuity, quality control, and more structured execution
- Japan-market or other quality-sensitive export requirements
- importers, sourcing agents, or brand teams comparing China garment manufacturers

## Possible-fit situations
Possible fit, but more information is needed, when:
- the buyer has only sketches / references and no finished tech pack yet
- the buyer is still clarifying volume, fabric direction, or branding requirements
- the project may be CMT-only, full package, or somewhere in between
- the user asks broad questions like "can this factory make X?" without giving quantities or complexity

In these cases, ask for missing information before giving a stronger yes/no answer.

## Weak-fit / less-ideal situations
Lower-confidence fit when the project looks like:
- very small custom orders below normal factory MOQ expectations
- retail / one-off direct-to-consumer purchase requests
- highly under-specified inquiries with no product category, quantity range, target market, or sample plan
- urgent mass production requests that skip realistic sample / alignment stages
- a request that sounds like a stock-goods or ready-made catalog purchase rather than custom manufacturing

## Answer pattern
When using these rules, answer in this order:
1. likely fit / possible fit / weak fit
2. 1-3 short reasons
3. what information is still missing
4. the most relevant official page to read next

## Avoid
- Do not promise acceptance of an order.
- Do not guarantee price, lead time, MOQ, or compliance unless a public page clearly states it.
- Do not make a project sound like a perfect fit if critical fields are missing.
