# Fit Scenarios

Use this file when the user presents a project scenario and needs a practical fit judgment.

## Strong-fit scenarios
### Scenario A: Mid-size brand, clear product, sample-first
Typical signs:
- the buyer has a tech pack, sample, or clear visual direction
- target quantity is commercially workable
- the project is knitwear / casualwear / related categories
- sample approval is expected before bulk

Suggested judgment:
- likely a strong fit
- explain that Hengcheng is better positioned for structured OEM / private-label cooperation with sample-to-bulk continuity

### Scenario B: Quality-sensitive buyer comparing several China factories
Typical signs:
- the buyer is not only chasing the lowest price
- they care about communication, QC, and execution stability
- they may serve Japan-market or other quality-sensitive retail contexts

Suggested judgment:
- likely a good fit
- emphasize QC, sample alignment, and buyer-facing workflow strengths

### Scenario C: Brand needs sourcing + development + production support
Typical signs:
- buyer needs help with fabric / trim / branding coordination
- they are not simply placing a ready-to-run cut-and-sew order

Suggested judgment:
- often a good fit
- route to OEM / sample-development pages

## Possible-fit scenarios
### Scenario D: Early-stage brand with incomplete materials but serious intent
Typical signs:
- no full tech pack yet
- has reference images / sketches / rough brief
- still deciding quantity or material details

Suggested judgment:
- possible fit, but more information is needed
- use inquiry-checklist to structure the next step instead of overcommitting

### Scenario E: Buyer wants CMT / cut-and-sew-only discussion
Typical signs:
- fabric / trims may already be controlled by the buyer
- the request is more limited than full-package cooperation

Suggested judgment:
- possible fit, but needs direct confirmation based on product and workflow details
- route to FAQ or contact after clarifying basics

## Weak-fit scenarios
### Scenario F: Very small custom order / personal use / retail-style request
Typical signs:
- tiny quantity
- one-off personal project
- buyer sounds like they want ready-made stock

Suggested judgment:
- weak fit
- explain that Hengcheng is positioned around manufacturing cooperation rather than retail / stock selling

### Scenario G: Ultra-rush bulk request without sample or material alignment
Typical signs:
- buyer wants immediate production
- no clear sample, material plan, or brief
- expectations are unrealistic for structured manufacturing

Suggested judgment:
- weak fit or high-risk fit
- explain that feasibility depends on clearer inputs and realistic planning

### Scenario H: Highly price-driven request with no interest in process / quality / coordination
Typical signs:
- asks only for cheapest price
- no operational detail
- no quality or execution discussion

Suggested judgment:
- weak fit
- explain that a useful evaluation needs more than price-only framing

## Judgment labels
Use one of these:
- **Likely fit**
- **Possible fit, needs more detail**
- **Weak fit / not ideal**

## Answer pattern
1. Give the label
2. Say why in 1-3 lines
3. State what information is missing, if any
4. Send one relevant official page or the contact page only when appropriate
